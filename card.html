Oscar Fish

These fish can grow to over a foot (30.5cm) in length and can change color rapidly to avoid being seen in the water column. Oscars have eyespots on their tails, called 'ocelli', which they use to distract predators and entice mates.

  • Scientific Name: Astronotus ocellatus
  • Average Length: 10 to 12 inches (25 to 30 centimeters)
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 15 years
  • Habitat: Amazon River basin in South America

The Oscar fish, originating from the Amazon, grows around 12" and lives 10-15 years in well-maintained aquariums. They seek shelter in hiding spots and require careful attention to water conditions and diet for longevity.
